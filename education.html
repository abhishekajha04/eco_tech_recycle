What is e-waste

E-waste is electronic products that are unwanted, not working , and nearly or at the end of their "Usefull life". Computers, Televisions, VCRS, Stereos, Copiers and Fax machines are everyday electronic products.E-waste is generic term used to decribe all types of old,end-of-life or discarded eletical and electronic equicment such as household appliances; office information and communication equipments; entertainment and consumer electronic equipment; lightning equipment; electrical and electronics tools

Harmful effects of e-waste
on humans

As mentioned electronic waste contains toxic components that are dangerous to human health such as mercury, lead, cadmium, polybrominated flames retardants, barium and lithium. The negetive health effects of these toxics on human includes brain, heart, liver, kidney, skeleton system damages. It can also considerebly affect the nervous and reproductive system of human bodies, leading to diseases and birth defects.

Harmful effects of e-waste
on air bodies

Contamination in the air occurs when e-waste is informally disposed of by dismantling, shredding, or melting the materials, releasing dust particles or toxins, such as dioxins, into the environment that cause air pollution and damage respiratory health.Chronic diseases and cancers are at a higher risk to occur when burning e-waste because it also releases fine particles, which can travel thousands of miles, creating numerous negative health risks to humans and animals.

Harmful effects of e-waste
on water bodies

When these heavy metals reach groundwater, they eventually make their way into ponds, streams, rivers and lakes. Through these pathways, acidification and toxification are created in the water, which is unsafe for animals, plants and communities even if they are miles away from a recycling site. Acidification can kill marine and freshwater organisms, disturb biodiversity and harm ecosystems. If acidification is present in water supplies, it can damage ecosystems to the point where recovery is questionable, if not impossible.
